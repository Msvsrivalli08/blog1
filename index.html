Welcome to our festival blog! Here we share some amazing festivals celebrated around the world, showcasing the colors, traditions, and unique celebrations of each.

Festivals table

Festival Name Date Location Description
Ganesh Chaturthi August/September India A Hindu festival celebrating the birth of Lord Ganesha, marked by the installation of Ganesh idols, prayers, and processions.
Diwali October/November India The Festival of Lights, symbolizing the victory of light over darkness.
Dasara September/October India Also known as Vijayadashami, it celebrates the victory of good over evil, specifically the victory of Lord Rama over Ravana.
Maha Shivaratri February/March India A Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Shiva, observed by fasting, night vigils, and prayers for blessings and spiritual awakening.
Sankranti January India A harvest festival celebrated in various parts of India, marking the transition of the Sun into Capricorn (Makara). It involves kite flying, traditional dances, and festive foods.
Ugadi March/April India (primarily Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Telangana) The New Year festival for the states of Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, and Telangana, celebrated with prayers, decorations, and traditional foods.
